Foundations

One accent. Royal Ink does the work of every accent role — links, focus rings, primary buttons, key data, the ink in the diagrams. Don't add a second hue. If you need emphasis beyond the accent, use weight, scale, or whitespace.

Vertical rhythm between sections is generous — 96 to 128px. The page should breathe. Tight spacing reads as utilitarian; this is study material, and the slow pace is the design.

02

Imagery

Two image systems do all the work. Stylized characters appear as visual punctuation between sections. Diagrams appear on loose-leaf paper, drawn in blue ballpoint and graphite.

Characters

Transparent PNGs on white paper. Never inside a card, never with a backdrop, never duplicated on the same screen. Placed by tag match from manifest.json — the character whose use_cases best fit the section. Their absurdity is the joke; the page around them stays straight-faced.

Diagrams · the signature element

Every conceptual diagram in Homeroom lives on a sheet of loose-leaf. Blue ballpoint for structure, pencil for asides. Annotations in a steady print hand. The paper itself does the work of differentiation — there is no other card style, no other background color, no other illustration treatment. When you see paper, you know you're looking at a model of something.

How a mortgage moves
↳ the loan doesn't stop at closing — it gets sold, pooled, sliced into MBS. that's the whole reason Pylon exists.

Diagram rules: ballpoint blue (#1E3A8A) for the diagram's spine — boxes, arrows, primary labels. Pencil graphite (#3F3F3F) for marginal notes, qualifications, asides. The hand uses Caveat at 18px for labels and 14px for notes. Slight rotation on asides (–2° to –4°) makes them feel placed, not typed. Don't add color past these two.

03

Components

A small library. Most pages are paragraphs and headings; the few interactive pieces are precise.

Buttons

Three roles: primary action, secondary action, navigation. No tertiary, no destructive. Labels say what happens, not what the system does. "Continue," not "Submit." "View answer key," not "Reveal."

Flashcard

Click to flip. One concept per card. The front is a question or term in display type; the back is the explanation in body type. Spaced-repetition queue is built on these.

Pop quizzes · three modes

The reader picks the mode in settings. Inline lives in the reading flow as a check-in. Gated blocks progress until passed. Practice lives in its own mode and never interrupts.

Inline check-in no progress effect
RESPA exempts loans under what dollar threshold when made for non-purchase purposes?
Correct. RESPA exempts small-dollar non-purchase loans at the $50,000 line. The threshold is one of three common RESPA exemptions to memorize, alongside business-purpose loans and vacant land.

Sits in the reading flow with a left rule in the accent color. Ungraded. Reveals the explanation on selection, then the reader continues.

Section checkpoint · question 3 of 5 4 to pass
The Loan Estimate must be delivered within how many business days of application?
Correct. Three business days from receipt of a complete application. The CD has its own timing — at least three business days before consummation. Don't confuse the two.

Light blue background distinguishes it from inline. Multiple questions stack; the reader has to clear a threshold to proceed.

Practice · randomized · timed 14 / 125 · 28:47 left
A borrower's monthly income is $8,000 and their total monthly debt is $2,400. What is their back-end DTI?
Correct. Back-end DTI is total monthly debt ÷ gross monthly income. $2,400 ÷ $8,000 = 30%. Front-end DTI uses only housing costs in the numerator.

SAFE-format mock exam mode. 125 questions, 190-minute timer, 75% to pass. Shadow instead of border. Never interrupts reading.

04

Page templates

Three template shapes account for nearly every screen.

Lesson

Eyebrow, big serif heading, prose, a loose-leaf diagram, more prose, a continue button. Single column, max-width readable line length. Most of the app is this.

Flashcard deck

One card in the center, deck progress at the bottom, the three controls underneath: again, hard, easy. The whole screen is one card.

Practice exam

Question counter top-left, timer top-right, question, four options stacked, next button bottom. Distraction-free. No nav, no character images.

05

Voice

Apply the de-llm-voice skill to every reader-facing string. The rules below are the ones that matter most for this domain.

don't
Let's dive into the fascinating world of mortgage origination!
do
An originator takes the borrower's application and walks the loan to closing.
don't
It's important to understand that TRID timing rules can be complex.
do
TRID has two timing rules. The LE goes out within three days of application. The CD lands at least three days before consummation.
don't
Great job! You got it right!
do
Correct.
don't
This section will explore the various aspects of RESPA compliance.
do
RESPA, in three rules.
don't
Don't worry if this is confusing — it's a tough topic!
do
The DTI calculation has a front-end version (housing only) and a back-end version (all debt). Most lenders look at the back end.
06

Don't

Things that would break the system if they crept in. Easy to slip on; cheap to catch.

A second accent color.Royal Ink does every accent job. Adding a second hue dilutes the signature and reads as a brand kit instead of a personality.
Backgrounds on character images.Every character PNG must be transparent. The white paper is the backdrop. Drop shadows, frames, and color fills behind characters all kill the gallery feel.
Loose-leaf paper for anything but diagrams.Don't put body text on the cream paper. Don't wrap quotes in it. The paper is the diagram's home and nothing else's — that's what makes the diagrams land.
Border radius on cards.The only rounded thing on screen is the brand mark and the flashcard's tiny 2px corners. Hairline borders, sharp corners. Rounded cards read as SaaS dashboard.
Gradients, shadows, glassmorphism.One drop-shadow exists on screen: the one under the loose-leaf paper. Nothing else casts a shadow. No glow effects, no gradient text.
Filler microcopy."Let's explore," "Did you know," "Pro tip," "Quick check." Cut all of it. Every line either says something true about the material or doesn't appear.
Stock icons or illustrations.The only illustrations are the tagged characters and the loose-leaf diagrams. No icon library, no spot illustrations, no decorative SVGs.
More than one character on a screen.The renderer picks one per section by tag match. Stacking two reads as a clip-art layout and makes both feel less considered.
